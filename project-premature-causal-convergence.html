Premature Causal Convergence in RCA

A pilot study of early-stage reasoning under closure pressure
closure pressure time-based observation pilot study timed reflection

In this pilot, first responses at the earliest timepoint were typically single-cause explanations. With additional time, broader contributors began to appear. Closure may operate less like a fully deliberate choice than like the early default of a pressured investigation environment.

This pilot provides the empirical basis for later intervention work by showing how single-cause reasoning can appear before broader contributors are explored — and why premature closure should be treated as a design problem as well as a cognitive one.

01

Background

Root Cause Analysis (RCA) is the formal process used to explain why a deviation occurred in regulated environments. It is often treated as a neutral reasoning task. In practice, however, deviation investigations unfold under expectations of speed, closure, and documentation readiness — conditions that shape how reasoning develops before broader contributing factors have been examined.

Investigators may document a root cause before fully examining procedural, contextual, or organizational contributors. This pilot observes this early-stage reasoning process directly, before any interface intervention.

Reasoning continues to develop — but documentation demands a commitment before it has time to.
02

Method

Participants received the same investigation-style case and explained the cause at three timepoints: immediately, after five minutes, and after ten minutes. The goal was to observe whether additional time changed the breadth, direction, or confidence of reasoning.

Designed and conducted a timed pilot reasoning study, including task construction, response comparison across timepoints, and pattern coding of causal breadth and revision.

Timepoint 01
0 minutes
Immediate explanation based on first interpretation of the case.
Timepoint 02
+5 minutes
Participants continued reflecting without additional prompts, allowing alternative contributors to emerge.
Timepoint 03
+10 minutes
Final reasoning state recorded to assess whether causal breadth changed further over time.
Rationale

Intentionally conducted without a prototype interface — establishing an empirical baseline for whether reasoning broadens over short intervals before any design intervention attempts to support it.

Implication

If reasoning naturally broadens with time, premature closure in investigation settings may reflect environmental pressure rather than fixed cognitive limitation.

03

Findings

Across participants, initial explanations were typically single-cause at the first timepoint. With additional time, broader contributors began to appear — including procedural, contextual, or organizational explanations that were absent from the first response, often alongside hesitation, self-correction, or explicit uncertainty.

By ten minutes, participants often broadened their explanation or became more explicit about uncertainty. The pattern suggests that early closure is shaped by constrained reasoning time rather than reflecting a fully developed explanation.

04

Results & Implications

This pilot does not claim statistical generalizability. Its contribution is observational: investigation reasoning changed meaningfully over short intervals, even without new information. That pattern matters because regulated documentation systems often demand closure during precisely that unstable period.

In regulated environments, early explanations are not just cognitive — they quickly become documented, audited, and consequential.

Empirical implication
  • Reasoning breadth is time-sensitive
  • Early explanations can underrepresent systemic contributors
  • Closure pressure can distort causal interpretation
Design implication
  • Investigation environments need structured support before closure
  • Reasoning scaffolds receive preliminary empirical support, not only conceptual motivation
  • Time-sensitive convergence is a UX problem
05

Connection to the Companion Project

This pilot is the empirical observation layer for the RCA Investigation Scaffolder. If premature convergence appears before broader contributors are considered, the design challenge becomes keeping investigation reasoning open longer without violating closure expectations.

Companion project
RCA Investigation Scaffolder
The companion project translates this observation into a structured interface intervention. In the scaffold study, many participants considered new causes after using the tool — suggesting that the convergence observed here may be at least partly addressable through design.

Paper

Claim

Premature causal convergence in GMP investigations appears to be shaped by closure pressure rather than individual error alone. Time-constrained reasoning can therefore be understood as a design problem, not only a cognitive one.

What it does
Provides an empirical baseline for convergence behavior in unassisted RCA reasoning — offering the observational foundation for the companion scaffold study.
