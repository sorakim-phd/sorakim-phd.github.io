// paper_01
document usability accessibility
SOP Accessibility and Compliance-Constrained Design
HCI paper · project-linked · SOP Accessibility

A paper on document accessibility, information retrievability, and compliance-constrained design in pharmaceutical quality control environments — developed from the SOP Accessibility project (N=12, 4.83/5, 100% adoption).

  • Argument: access failure is a judgment failure — workers who cannot find the right SOP cannot apply it correctly.
  • Design contribution: usability improvements can emerge through external support layers without modifying validated source files.
  • Positions document accessibility as a substantive HCI problem in regulated environments.

// paper_02
reasoning support investigation design
Reasoning Under Closure Pressure: Scaffolding Root Cause Analysis in Regulated Environments
HCI paper · two-project pair · RCA + PCC

Combines two studies: a pilot observation of premature causal convergence in unassisted investigation reasoning (N=5), and a usability study of a scaffold designed to counter it (N=15).

  • Pilot: 5/5 began with a single cause — revised toward systemic explanations only when given unstructured time.
  • Scaffold: 80% increased causal breadth · all 5 who started with individual blame revised toward systemic factors.
  • Argument: investigation quality is a cognitive systems design problem, not a training problem.
Paper direction active · N=5 pilot + N=15 usability data collected
→ RCA project → PCC project
// paper_03
document navigation traceability
Document Navigation as a Design Problem: Traceability and Behavioral Friction in GMP Environments
HCI paper · two-project pair · Cross-Ref + Navigation

Combines two projects: a metadata-driven cross-reference system addressing structural traceability gaps (N=17), and a behavioral navigation tracking prototype that makes invisible navigation friction visible (N=5).

  • Cross-ref: 30–60% faster task completion · 2× document discovery · path completion 30–50% → 70–90%.
  • Navigation: 5/5 loopback · less experienced users identified SOP structure — not the interface — as friction source.
  • Argument: where a practitioner loops back is a signal the document structure failed, not the user.
Paper direction active · N=17 + N=5 usability data collected
→ cross-ref project → navigation project

Workflow Friction and Observability in Deviation–CAPA Review Cycles
HCI paper · project-linked · Review Dashboard

A paper on review interaction, workflow friction, and observability in validated pharmaceutical quality review systems — developed from the Smart Feedback and Review Dashboard prototype (N=6).

  • Problem: quality review cycles are opaque — workload imbalance and delay accumulation are invisible until they have already become a problem.

// paper_05 · research backbone
regulatory UX concept paper writing now
Regulatory UX: Toward a Human-Centered Framework for Compliance-Constrained Interaction
HCI concept paper · umbrella framework · connects all six projects

A concept paper introducing Regulatory UX as a new HCI research frame — the study of worker interaction experience inside compliance-constrained systems, where procedural documents, audit workflows, and investigation structures actively shape navigation, cognition, and decision-making.

  • Core argument: regulated documentation systems are not neutral containers for compliance information — they are active interaction environments that shape how workers search, reason, assign blame, and close investigations.
  • Framework contribution: Regulatory UX names and structures a design space that existing HCI frameworks have not systematically addressed — where usability, compliance, and accountability intersect.
  • Connects all six projects: positions the portfolio as a coherent empirical program instantiating the Regulatory UX argument across multiple failure modes.
Currently writing · umbrella paper for the full research program

Anticipated Accountability Convergence
STS conceptual manuscript
PDF ↗

Develops a structural theory of how accountability expectations become aligned before action through the convergence of procedural rules, documentation regimes, and evaluative infrastructures.

  • Focus: how organizations preconfigure what counts as accountable conduct before execution begins.
  • HCI relevance: explains why design interventions must account for prospective accountability structures — not just usability at the moment of use.

// RC

Responsibility Convergence in Organizational Investigations
STS conceptual manuscript
PDF ↗

Explains why organizational investigations like root cause analysis recurrently stabilize accountability at identifiable actors even when failures are distributed across sociotechnical systems.

  • Core mechanisms: procedural ambiguity, attributional closure pressure, and accountability legibility bias.
  • HCI relevance: grounds the RCA Reasoning Scaffolder and Premature Causal Convergence projects — explains the structural conditions the scaffold is designed to counter.
after events → causal ambiguity is translated into administratively actionable responsibility

Premature Accountability Convergence
STS conceptual manuscript
PDF ↗

Analyzes how AI-mediated representations reshape the timing and scope of responsibility stabilization by compressing interpretive space and accelerating convergence within accountability infrastructures.
