About

I study how responsibility becomes structured, stabilized, and accelerated within organizational systems. My work focuses on how documentation, procedural infrastructures, and computational representations shape the way accountability emerges in regulated sociotechnical environments.

Research Focus

My research examines the organizational mechanisms through which responsibility becomes aligned before action, embedded within procedural structures during execution, stabilized through investigative processes after events, and increasingly reshaped through computational mediation.

Across highly regulated environments such as pharmaceuticals and other compliance-intensive industries, accountability rarely emerges spontaneously. Instead, it is produced through structured documentation regimes, procedural texts, investigative infrastructures, and evaluative systems that define what counts as responsible action.

Research Program

My current research program develops a theoretical framework describing how responsibility evolves across the organizational lifecycle.

The program includes four connected conceptual components:

  • Anticipated Accountability Convergence (AAC) — how accountability expectations align before action.
  • Cognitive Friction in Procedural Texts (CF) — how procedural language distributes interpretive burden during execution.
  • Responsibility Convergence (RC) — how investigations stabilize responsibility after events.
  • Premature Accountability Convergence (PAC) — how AI-mediated representations accelerate responsibility stabilization.

Background

My professional background is in pharmaceutical quality systems, where I worked extensively with regulated documentation, method verification processes, and organizational investigation structures. This experience informs my interest in how documentation architectures and accountability systems shape organizational reasoning.

Building on this experience, my research explores the sociotechnical dynamics of responsibility across documentation systems, investigative processes, and AI-mediated infrastructures.
