About

My work asks a simple question: when something goes wrong in a high-stakes organization, how does responsibility get made — and who (or what) ends up carrying it?

Research story

I come from regulated work settings where decisions are made under procedures, audits, timelines, and recurring documentation demands. In these environments, “responsibility” is not just a moral label — it is produced through forms, protocols, investigation routines, and the pressure to reach closure.

That experience shaped my research direction: I study responsibility-making as a sociotechnical process. I focus on how responsibility becomes structured, stabilized, and sometimes accelerated through procedural infrastructures and AI-mediated representations.

I describe my professional background at a high level on purpose; the work is best captured as regulated quality systems and audit-facing documentation practices.

What I’m building
  • Conceptual frameworks for responsibility across stages of work (before action, during investigation, after closure).
  • Text- and procedure-centered analyses of how judgment is guided inside documentation.
  • Design-relevant accounts of how AI can reshape what becomes legible, assignable, and actionable.
