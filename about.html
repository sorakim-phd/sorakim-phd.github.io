About

I study how responsibility is produced under constraint in regulated work.

My work sits at the intersection of HCI and STS, centered on how documentation, workflow structure, and closure pressure shape judgment in high-stakes environments. I am not interested in reducing regulation; I am interested in how systems can be redesigned so responsibility remains visible, reasoning remains open, and accountability is better supported inside formal constraints.

Why this work

I have spent more than four years working inside pharmaceutical quality systems, where documentation, review, and investigation are tightly coupled to accountability. In that setting, I saw the same pattern repeatedly: when work became difficult, structural burdens were often translated into individual responsibility problems.

That experience shaped the question behind my research. Rather than treating responsibility as something neutrally assigned after the fact, I study how it converges through procedures, documents, and time pressure — especially under conditions of closure pressure, where investigations settle quickly and accountability narrows.

To investigate this, I designed a series of empirical HCI projects grounded in pharmaceutical quality environments. These projects examine how navigation burden, traceability gaps, review opacity, and reasoning structure shape judgment — and how alternative designs can preserve comparison, delay premature closure, and expand accountable reasoning.

Research mode
HCI-centered empirical projects with STS grounding on accountability and formal systems.
Design position
Human-centered support without collapsing judgment into automation.
Core question
How does responsibility settle?

I study how responsibility becomes visible, documentable, and narrowed through system design — not only through individual decisions.

Empirical strategy
Six HCI projects in regulated environments

The projects do not treat usability as the endpoint. They ask what kind of burden a tool makes visible, and what that reveals about judgment and accountability in practice.

Research stance
Regulation stays.

The point is not to loosen formal systems, but to redesign interaction conditions so workers can reason more openly inside them.

Research identity
How I position the work
Main agenda
Responsibility under constraint

My central research question is how responsibility is produced under conditions structured by documentation, workflow, and closure pressure. I study those conditions as sociotechnical arrangements, not merely as individual reasoning problems.

core orientation · responsibility, closure, accountability
HCI contribution
Judgment-preserving design

I build lightweight external systems, scaffolds, and visibility layers that support navigation, comparison, and causal reasoning without replacing human judgment or altering validated source systems.

STS grounding
Why these frictions matter

STS helps explain why navigation burden, procedural ambiguity, and investigative closure are not surface issues, but structural conditions through which accountability is organized.

What ties it together
