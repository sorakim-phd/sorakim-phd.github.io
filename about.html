About

I’m an independent researcher working at the intersection of organizational accountability, procedural infrastructures, and high-stakes decision environments.

My empirical grounding comes from pharmaceutical quality systems, where documentation, investigation routines, and compliance pressures continuously shape how responsibility is produced, stabilized, and made legible.

I develop frameworks that connect stages across the responsibility lifecycle: anticipatory alignment (AAC), procedural judgment structuring (Cognitive Friction), investigative stabilization (Responsibility Convergence), and computational acceleration (AI-Mediated Responsibility / PAC).
