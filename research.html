Responsibility, made.

My work maps how responsibility becomes structured, stabilized, and sometimes accelerated inside high-stakes sociotechnical organizations. I focus on regulated systems where procedures, investigations, and documentation do not merely record decisions — they actively shape what becomes accountable.

AAC anticipation accountability pressure
Anticipated Accountability Convergence (AAC)

AAC theorizes how accountability gets “pre-compressed” before action: people align their judgments, documentation, and justifications in advance, anticipating auditability, review, and downstream explanation demands. In regulated settings, this anticipation is not optional — it becomes infrastructure.

  • Mechanism: anticipatory alignment under procedural time + review pressure.
  • Empirical hook: documentation that is written for future scrutiny, not present clarity.
  • Contribution: accountability as a temporal process (before action), not only a post-hoc assignment.
anticipation + procedural time → pre-structured accountability
CF procedural text cognitive + organizational friction
SOP Cognitive Friction (CF)

CF examines how procedural documents shape work by creating friction: not just “difficulty,” but structured pauses, uncertainty, and decision bottlenecks. I treat SOPs as coordination artifacts that distribute interpretive burden — and therefore redistribute responsibility.

  • Measure: text structure + step complexity + decision points (where judgment is forced).
  • Outcome: where errors become likely, and where accountability becomes assignable.
  • Contribution: responsibility isn’t only moral; it’s engineered through document structure.
text structure → interpretive burden → assignable responsibility
RC investigations blame / convergence
Responsibility Convergence (RC)

RC explains how investigations narrow a complex sociotechnical failure into a legible, reportable account — often converging toward a person or a single proximate cause. The focus is not “bias” as a trait, but convergence as an organizational closure mechanism.

  • Mechanism: legibility demands + closure pressure + documentation constraints.
  • Bridge: from RCA tools to broader organizational accountability dynamics.
  • Contribution: how “structural problems” get narratively stabilized as “someone’s fault.”
schematic: convergence from distributed signals toward a single accountable narrative.
PAC AI mediation accountability legibility
Procedural Accountability & AI Mediation (PAC)

PAC describes how responsibility becomes made “machine-legible” through representations: templates, trackers, risk tables, and AI-assisted summaries. I examine how these tools stabilize accounts — and what gets lost when accountability must fit a format.

  • Question: when AI structures the narrative, who becomes accountable for the story?
  • Mechanism: formatting + summarization + category constraints → legibility bias.
  • Contribution: AI as accountability infrastructure (not merely decision support).
formatting constraints → legibility → stabilized accountability
