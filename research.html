main agenda

Human-centered systems for high-stakes procedural work

My research centers on how documentation, workflows, and support tools shape judgment in environments where people must work carefully, defensibly, and under strong formal constraints. I approach these as HCI problems: usability, interpretation, visibility, and reasoning support.

core orientation: designing systems that support judgment without flattening complexity
supporting lens

Regulatory UX and procedural cognition

I study how people interpret instructions, manage ambiguity, and navigate cognitively demanding documentation in regulated environments.

supporting lens

Responsibility, interpretation, and closure

I use conceptual work on accountability and interpretive closure to better understand what support systems reveal, compress, or prematurely stabilize.

core questions

Core research questions

from documents to decision support
question 01

How do procedural systems shape judgment?

I ask how documentation structures, review formats, and workflow environments distribute interpretive burden across workers in regulated settings.

question 02

Where does cognitive friction emerge?

I study where people pause, loop back, hesitate, or reconstruct meaning when written procedure and actual reasoning diverge.

question 03

How can support tools help without oversimplifying?

I explore support layers, scaffolds, and interface interventions that improve navigation and reasoning while preserving interpretive openness.

question 04

What changes under AI mediation?

I investigate how AI systems may accelerate closure, compress ambiguity, and reshape accountability in high-stakes procedural environments.

methods

Methods

qualitative, computational, and design-oriented
qualitative

Document analysis, observation, and close reading

I use document analysis, qualitative coding, and close attention to workflow structure to understand how people actually work through regulated documentation, review, and investigative processes.

computational / design

Metadata analysis, visualization, and interaction prototyping

I use Excel and Python to structure metadata, analyze workflow patterns, visualize procedural systems, and build lightweight interaction prototypes around judgment, coordination, and reasoning support.

conceptual backbone

Theory supporting the broader HCI agenda

grounding, not a separate track

Alongside design-oriented and project-based work, I develop conceptual frameworks that clarify how judgment, interpretation, responsibility, and closure operate in high-stakes procedural environments. These frameworks are part of the intellectual grounding of my HCI research, not a separate track away from it.

AAC
Anticipated Accountability Convergence
before action · interpretive expectations

AAC examines how expectations of defensible action become aligned before work begins.

prospective accountability and interpretation
CF
Cognitive Friction
during execution · procedural cognition

CF examines how procedural text structure creates localized judgment demands during execution.

documents, cognition, and procedural usability
RC
Responsibility Convergence
after events · investigative closure

RC explains why investigations often stabilize responsibility through administratively legible outcomes.

review systems and investigative reasoning
PAC
Preemptive Accountability Convergence
AI mediation · decision closure

PAC studies how AI-mediated systems may accelerate convergence before ambiguity has been meaningfully examined.

decision support, AI mediation, and accountability timing
