Sora Kim

I study how responsibility becomes structured, stabilized, and accelerated in high-stakes sociotechnical organizations. My work examines procedural infrastructures, investigative routines, and AI-mediated representations in regulated systems.

About

I am a researcher focused on responsibility and accountability in regulated organizational systems. Drawing from pharmaceutical quality environments, I develop theoretical frameworks explaining how responsibility is anticipated before action, stabilized during investigation, and intensified through computational mediation.

Research Program

My work advances a unified responsibility lifecycle framework spanning: anticipatory alignment (AAC), procedural judgment structuring (Cognitive Friction), investigative stabilization (Responsibility Convergence), and computational acceleration (AI-Mediated Responsibility / PAC).
