Sora Kim
HCI · STS · AI & Accountability in Regulated Work

Responsibility converges under pressure.

In regulated investigations, responsibility often converges too early. I study how documentation, workflow, and AI-supported scaffolds shape which explanations stay visible long enough to be compared before closure settles.

I treat AI not as a documentation shortcut, but as a reasoning intervention: preserving alternatives, delaying premature convergence, and reshaping how responsibility is formed in high-stakes quality work.

Research framing

Responsibility is not given — it is produced through system structure under constraint.

Empirical anchor Root cause investigations in GMP-regulated work
Primary mechanism Closure pressure that narrows causal reasoning and concentrates accountability
AI direction Scaffolds that preserve comparison and uncertainty instead of merely accelerating paperwork
Conceptual core
A linked research program on how responsibility is produced through documentation, investigation, interface structure, and AI-supported reasoning.
These projects function as one inquiry into accountable reasoning, not as separate tools.
Empirical approach
Practitioner-based pilot studies examining when explanations broaden or narrow under closure pressure, structured prompting, and AI-supported comparison.
The key outcome is not only usability, but whether responsibility settles too quickly onto a single account.
Design contribution
Intervention prototypes that preserve comparison, surface uncertainty, and keep constrained reasoning revisable before responsibility prematurely converges.
Here, AI functions as a mechanism for widening accountable reasoning rather than accelerating paperwork.

About
Why responsibility narrows too quickly in regulated work.
I have spent over four years working in pharmaceutical quality control within GMP-regulated environments. That experience shaped the core question behind my research: not whether regulation should be weakened, but why responsibility narrows so quickly in regulated work — and how systems can remain rigorous while giving practitioners more room to compare, question, and justify competing explanations before closure hardens into blame.
