human-centered systems · regulated environments

I study how regulated systems
shape human judgment.

In pilot and task-based studies (N=52) across pharmaceutical QC and regulated work settings, I examine how procedural systems constrain causal reasoning under time and interface pressure — combining usability testing, behavioral trace analysis, and prototype interventions. In one pilot study, all five investigators initially converged on a single cause, revising toward systemic explanations only when given structured time and support, highlighting how design can expand reasoning pathways and make alternative explanations visible.

SOP Accessibility System

A retrieval-focused interface for navigating procedural documents. Evaluated through task-based experiments (N=12), showing reduced retrieval time (6–7 min → 1–2 min) and changes in navigation behavior under constrained search conditions.

// featured_02
RCA Reasoning Scaffolder

A reasoning scaffold designed to counter premature causal convergence in investigation workflows. In pilot usability testing (N=15), 80% of participants expanded causal breadth, with all initially individual-blame hypotheses revised toward systemic explanations.

reasoning investigation N=15 live demo
// featured_03
Cognitive Navigation Explorer

A behavioral tracking prototype examining how users traverse document networks under constrained visibility. In a task-based study (N=5), all participants revisited at least one document, suggesting that navigation structure directly shapes attention allocation and judgment pathways.
