Hi 🙌 I study how responsibility becomes structured.

I study how responsibility becomes structured, stabilized, and accelerated in high-stakes sociotechnical organizations through procedural documentation, investigative routines, and AI-mediated representations in regulated systems, combining conceptual work with corpus-based and document-centered empirical analysis.

status: Preparing PhD applications for Fall 2027 (U.S.) · Interests: sociotechnical systems, organizational accountability, infrastructure studies, and information governance.

# quick spec focus: responsibility-making in regulated organizations research program: AAC → CF → RC → PAC methods: theory + procedural text corpora + qualitative document analysis empirical tracks: 1) CF corpus expansion + annotation reliability testing 2) RC pattern analysis in FDA Warning Letters / Form 483 to extend PAC

Current obsession

How accountability becomes legible, actionable, and prematurely stabilized under procedural and investigative closure pressure.

Empirical grounding

Pharmaceutical quality systems and regulatory infrastructures (SOP documentation, deviations, CAPA/RCA investigations, and audit or inspection-facing records).

CF empirical extension

Expanding the Cognitive Friction study through a larger corpus of procedural and regulatory texts, with annotation reliability testing and feature co-occurrence analysis in progress.

PAC empirical extension

Analyzing responsibility convergence patterns in FDA Warning Letters and Form 483 materials to extend the PAC framework through regulatory document analysis.
