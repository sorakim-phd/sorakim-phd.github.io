Designing systems
for complex work.

My work sits across HCI, regulatory UX, procedural cognition, and organizational systems. I study how documents, workflows, interfaces, and support structures shape navigation, reasoning, interpretation, and decision-making in high-stakes environments.

research-program direction

What I study

I am interested in how procedural environments structure thought: how people move through documents, how they interpret evidence, how workflows narrow or expand judgment, and how support systems shape decision closure.

Where it sits

This work draws on HCI and UX, but also on broader questions about systems, organizational environments, infrastructure, accountability, and human judgment under constraint.

current build

Designing support systems for reasoning in high-stakes procedural work

I explore how interface structures, metadata layers, workflow visibility, and reasoning scaffolds can make complex work more navigable without flattening interpretation or replacing human judgment.

bridge

From HCI and UX to broader systems questions

My projects begin with usability and interaction, but extend toward larger questions about how institutions, infrastructures, and procedural systems organize accountability, closure, and decision-making.

domain

Regulated documentation as a living user environment

SOPs, investigations, CAPA records, and inspection-facing documents are not only compliance artifacts. They are environments people inhabit while trying to find, interpret, justify, and act.

orientation

Not just better interfaces, but better conditions for thought

I am interested in systems that support clearer reasoning, slower premature closure, more legible workflows, and more humane decision processes under procedural pressure.
