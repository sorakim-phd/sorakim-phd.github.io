01

Problem

Root Cause Analysis (RCA) in GMP investigations is intended to explain why a deviation occurred, but closure pressure often narrows reasoning before alternatives are explored.

This project examines a pattern consistent with Premature Accountability Convergence (PAC): investigation reasoning narrowing toward an individual actor before procedural, contextual, and organizational contributors have been examined. The claim here is not only about cognitive bias, but also about interface and workflow design.

Investigation pressure points
  • Closure pressure favors fast accountability over broad exploration
  • Standard forms underrepresent alternative causal pathways
  • Reasoning is documented late, after convergence has already occurred
  • Interface structure reinforces single-path explanation habits
Design implication

If investigation environments structure reasoning, responsibility attribution may partly reflect interaction conditions. The question is not only who is blamed, but how the system made one attribution path easier to follow than others.

02

Method

01
Investigation structure analysis

Analyzed recurring RCA patterns in GMP deviation workflows, focusing on early blame convergence, missing alternative contributors, and reasoning compression under closure pressure.

02
Scaffold design

Designed a five-step reasoning flow separating intake, hypothesis generation, PAC analysis, structured reasoning, and pre-closure checks to slow premature convergence.

03
PAC detection + AI reasoning expansion

Built rule-based PAC detection to flag individual-blame language and surface alternative pathways. Integrated AI as a reasoning expansion layer within Step 3 — not to generate answers, but to reopen consideration when convergence risk appears.

04
Comparative think-aloud study + post-task ratings · N=20

Compared participants' initial causal interpretations with post-scaffold reasoning, observing whether structured support changed breadth of explanation, confidence, and closure timing.

03

UI Prototype

A compliance-safe RCA interface that preserves causal breadth during deviation investigations. The goal is not to automate conclusions — it is to scaffold reasoning before closure.

04

Reasoning Expansion

AI in investigation contexts is often imagined as a way to accelerate decision-making and compress ambiguity. This system takes the opposite premise. The AI layer expands interpretive space when convergence risk appears in Step 3, acting as a resistance mechanism rather than a decision engine.

That distinction matters because the design goal here is not better answer production. It is better reasoning conditions. Instead of proposing a final cause, the model is constrained to reopen comparison, surface missing contributors, and keep evidentiary questions alive before closure.

Integrated into Step 3 (PAC Analysis) of the scaffold — not a separate tool. Activates when individual-blame language is detected and does not produce a final-answer output.

What it does
  • Detects early convergence toward individual-blame language
  • Surfaces alternative contributors not yet considered
  • Generates reopening questions and comparison prompts
  • Highlights evidence to examine before closure
Design principle

AI is introduced not to suggest causes but to reopen possibility. The model's role is to expand, not compress — preserving human interpretive authority while countering PAC rather than intensifying it.

This design uses AI to resist premature closure, not to accelerate it. The point is to widen reasoning space long enough for alternatives, evidence, and comparison to stay visible.
05

Comparative Study

Participants first converged on a single cause without support, then revisited the same case using the scaffold. Structured support was associated with broader reasoning and later closure.

These results are exploratory and session-based. They indicate design promise, but they do not by themselves establish long-term investigation quality or downstream accountability outcomes.

06

What This Revealed

Investigation environments do not simply record reasoning — they also help shape it. Under closure pressure, participants often moved quickly toward an accountable actor even when broader contributors remained plausible.

Participants with the most GMP experience showed little reasoning change, suggesting the scaffold may be most useful when investigation habits are still forming or when users are more open to structured comparison.

07

Paper

Claim

Investigation environments can be redesigned to keep reasoning space open longer without undermining compliance expectations. Interface structure may influence how causal reasoning takes shape in regulated investigations.

What it does
Uses PAC as a conceptual frame to examine how interface structure influences causal reasoning in regulated investigations. Argues that a lightweight scaffold — and an AI layer constrained to expand rather than resolve reasoning — can resist closure pressure without modifying validated systems.
