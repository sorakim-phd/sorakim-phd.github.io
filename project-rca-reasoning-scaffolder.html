RCA Reasoning Scaffolder

Investigation reasoning is not a linear process — but regulated systems pressure it to become one.

// 01

Problem

In Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) environments, Root Cause Analysis (RCA) is the formal process that connects a deviation to its underlying causes. In practice, that process faces a structural tension: human reasoning is exploratory and branching, but regulatory documentation expects a linear, stable explanation.

The result is Premature Accountability Convergence (PAC) — investigation reasoning narrows toward an individual actor before procedural, contextual, and organizational contributors have been fully examined. This isn't just a cognitive bias; it's a design problem.

// observed patterns
  • Investigations converged on one explanation early
  • Alternative hypotheses disappeared once documentation turned linear
  • Causal reasoning stayed internal — hard to externalize
  • Contributing factors compressed out during narrative write-up
// design constraint

The challenge was not to replace the official RCA system — validated GMP workflows cannot be modified. The goal was to support better reasoning around those systems, without touching them.

// 02

Method

01
RCA structure mapping

Mapped typical GMP investigation workflows and 5-Whys logic to identify where causal paths tend to narrow too quickly — before systemic contributors have been examined.

02
scaffold design

Designed a step-by-step reasoning scaffold prompting investigators to maintain multiple hypotheses, attach contributing factors and evidence per path, and track closure pressure in real time.

03
PAC detection + AI integration

Built rule-based PAC detection to flag individual-blame language and surface alternative causal pathways. Integrated an AI expansion layer — not to generate answers, but to keep reasoning space open when convergence risk is detected.

04
usability study · N=15, pre/post protocol

Conducted a structured study measuring reasoning breadth change, interface usability, and accountability attribution — before and after scaffold use — with GMP-experienced participants.

// 03

Prototype

A 5-step investigation scaffold: Intake → Hypotheses → PAC Analysis → Reasoning → Pre-Closure. Each step is designed to resist convergence — keeping multiple causal paths visible before committing to a final explanation.

// 04

AI Integration

Most AI tools applied to investigation tasks are designed to converge — they rank causes by probability, recommend a single CAPA, or summarize the most likely explanation. In regulated environments, this accelerates PAC: the first plausible answer becomes the default, and accountability stabilizes before systemic contributors have been examined.

The AI layer in this prototype is designed to do the opposite. It is integrated directly into Step 3 (PAC Analysis) and activated only when convergence risk is detected — not as a default output, but as a targeted intervention.

// why AI was added
  • Rule-based PAC detection catches explicit blame language
  • AI expansion surfaces contextual and organizational pathways rule-based logic may miss
  • Reopening questions prompt investigators to ask what would need to be true for the current hypothesis to be wrong
  • Counter-PAC design: AI widens reasoning space rather than narrowing it
// what AI does not do
  • Does not output a final root cause
  • Does not make compliance decisions
  • Does not replace human judgment or documented RCA
  • Does not activate unless convergence risk is present — not a default layer
When AI systems generate single-cause explanations in regulated environments, they do not just summarize — they accelerate accountability convergence. This prototype explores what happens when AI is designed to resist that tendency instead.
// design caution
// known limitations
  • Rule-based PAC detection may miss indirect blame language or implicit framing
  • AI-generated pathways are structurally informed, not domain-verified — require investigator judgment
  • Prototype uses synthetic GMP scenarios; real-world validation pending
// future direction
  • Domain-specific PAC term expansion beyond explicit blame language
  • Longitudinal study of reasoning patterns across repeated scaffold use
  • Evaluation in live deviation investigation contexts

// 06

What This Revealed

Investigation quality is not only a function of domain expertise — it is also shaped by the reasoning environment. Regulated systems that prioritize rapid closure create structural pressure toward PAC before systemic contributors have been examined.

This study provides empirical evidence that a lightweight scaffold can resist that pressure. The three participants who reported no reasoning change were those with the most extensive GMP investigation experience — suggesting that scaffolding value may be highest earlier in career development, when investigation patterns are still forming.

documentation pressure → premature convergence → individual blame → systemic factors invisible

The central argument: PAC is not only a cognitive bias but a structural feature of regulated investigation systems, and it can be partially addressed through compliance-safe design interventions.
