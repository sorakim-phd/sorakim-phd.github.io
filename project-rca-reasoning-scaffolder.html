🧩 Background

problem framing

In Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) environments, deviations require structured investigations to identify root causes and determine corrective and preventive actions. These investigations are typically documented through Root Cause Analysis (RCA).

In practice, investigators must connect multiple contributing conditions — procedures, equipment states, environmental factors, and operator actions — while producing a clear written explanation suitable for quality review and regulatory documentation.

This creates a tension between how reasoning actually unfolds and how investigation narratives are expected to be written.

💬 Human reasoning is often exploratory and branching, while procedural documentation expects a linear and stable explanation.

⚠️ Observation

reasoning friction

Observed pain points

  • Investigations frequently converged on a single explanation early in the reasoning process.
  • Alternative causal hypotheses became harder to maintain once documentation turned linear.
  • Causal reasoning was largely internal and difficult to externalize.
  • Complex contributing factors could disappear during narrative compression.

Why this was difficult

In a regulated GMP environment, investigation quality depends not only on domain knowledge but also on how well reasoning can be structured under documentation pressure. The challenge was not “change the official RCA system,” but rather “support better reasoning without touching validated documentation workflows.”

🎯 Design question

HCI framing

How can a lightweight reasoning scaffold help investigators preserve broader causal exploration in GMP deviation analysis while remaining compatible with validated documentation practices?

🧠 Method

low-code + qualitative

1. RCA structure mapping

Mapped typical RCA reasoning structures using common investigation templates and 5-Whys logic to identify where explanation paths tend to narrow too quickly.

2. Lightweight scaffold design

Designed a reasoning scaffold that prompted investigators to consider alternative hypotheses and parallel contributing conditions before settling on a final explanatory path.

3. Causal diagram support

Introduced simple causal diagrams to externalize intermediate reasoning steps and make branching pathways more visible during investigation work.

4. Qualitative evaluation

Conducted qualitative evaluation of whether the scaffold helped structure investigation thinking and support broader causal reasoning during deviation analysis.

🧪 Evaluation logic

prototype evaluation

Evaluation setup

Because validated GMP documentation systems cannot be casually modified, the scaffold was framed as a lightweight support layer rather than a replacement tool. Evaluation focused on whether it improved the quality and breadth of reasoning before final documentation closure.

investigation task scaffold support formal RCA write-up

What was examined

  • whether alternative hypotheses remained visible longer
  • whether contributing factors were more clearly articulated
  • whether reasoning became easier to externalize
  • whether the scaffold reduced narrative oversimplification

Why this matters

The point was not to challenge GMP compliance. It was to ask a more human-centered question: if investigators had a better reasoning aid before writing the final RCA narrative, would the investigation process itself become more robust and less prematurely narrowed?

💬 Main takeaway

The key value of the scaffold is not that it changes the official RCA format, but that it supports better reasoning before the final explanation is compressed into that format.

📄 Paper in progress

research output

This project can also be developed into a paper on cognitive-pathway modeling, procedural mismatch, and human reasoning under regulatory constraint.

The broader paper contribution is that procedural systems are written as linear sequences, while human reasoning unfolds as adaptive path structures. The gap between written procedure and actual cognitive navigation is therefore measurable — and should be treated as a design problem rather than merely a user problem.

💬 Reflection

why it is HCI

This project reframes investigation quality as a cognitive systems design issue rather than purely an individual skill problem. In other words, weak investigations are not always the result of weak investigators; they can also emerge from reasoning environments that encourage premature linearization.

From an HCI perspective, the important insight is that supporting structured exploration of causal pathways can improve investigation reasoning without compromising regulatory documentation requirements.
