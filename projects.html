How regulated systems narrow reasoning — and how that narrowing can be restructured.

Built from pharmaceutical QC practice. These projects examine how regulated systems narrow reasoning, shift interpretive burden, and shape what stays visible long enough to support accountable judgment — while keeping human judgment at the center.

Expanding Causal Reasoning under Closure Pressure

Premature causal convergence in GMP investigations can be shaped by closure pressure: reasoning may narrow before alternative contributors are fully explored.

A structured reasoning intervention that keeps alternative explanations visible long enough to delay premature convergence without automating the conclusion.

Scaffolded use delayed closure and expanded the range of comparable explanations.

Premature Convergence under Closure Pressure

Early closure is not always deliberate; time pressure and the need for a quickly documentable explanation can push reasoning toward a single cause.

A companion empirical study examining how single-cause reasoning broadens when investigators are given more time and interpretive space before closure.

Given more time and interpretive space, explanations broadened beyond an initial single-cause account.

Procedural Visibility in SOP Navigation

Document inaccessibility creates interpretive burden upstream of formal error, turning retrieval difficulty into later judgment difficulty and narrowing what can be compared in practice.

An external retrieval intervention that makes scanned SOP archives searchable without altering validated systems, increasing procedural visibility without changing validated workflows.

The intervention reduced the time required to access comparison-relevant procedures, making them more immediately comparable at the point of need.
document UX · retrieval · streamlit · python →

Traceability gaps are not merely maintenance defects; they redistribute hidden connective labor onto practitioners.

A traceability intervention that makes document linkage, broken paths, and lifecycle mismatches visible across document networks.

Cross-reference visibility made document paths more directly traceable at the point of use.

Navigation Friction and Loopback Behavior

Loopbacks are not simply user error; they can mark moments where document structure failed to resolve a judgment demand on the first pass.

A behavioral observation intervention that captures navigation paths, hesitation, and cross-reference jumps to make otherwise hidden document friction observable.

Loopbacks surfaced structural friction and moments of navigation hesitation across sessions.
